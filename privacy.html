Privacy policy

Static surface

This privacy policy explains how StreamSuites™ collects, uses, discloses, and protects information across our public pages, creator/admin dashboards, runtime-connected services, and operational tooling. It applies to visitors, creators, subscribers, and end users who may interact with StreamSuites-enabled experiences.

Last updated: February 9, 2026

Who we are and what this policy covers

StreamSuites™ is operated by Brainstream Media Group. This policy covers information processed when you: visit our public surfaces (including streamsuites.app), use our dashboards (including creator/admin surfaces), authenticate and connect third-party accounts, configure a StreamSuites runtime, receive communications from us, make payments or donations, or subscribe to operational status updates.

  • Visitors: people who browse public pages without signing in.
  • Creators / Account holders: people who register and use dashboards and runtime features.
  • End users: people who interact with StreamSuites-enabled livestreams, chat automations, or shared content (even without a StreamSuites account).
  • Status subscribers: people who subscribe to service status notifications.

If a specific product surface displays a different privacy notice, that notice will govern for that surface.

Information we collect

The categories below describe information we may collect or receive depending on how you use StreamSuites.

  • Account and profile data: email address, display name, internal identifiers, role/permissions, and account preferences.
  • Login and security data: login timestamps, IP address, device/browser details (user agent), session identifiers, and security/abuse signals.
  • Credentials: if direct login is enabled, passwords are stored only as salted hashes (not plaintext).
  • OAuth and connected account data: provider identifiers and basic account info returned by OAuth providers (such as a provider user ID; and email/username only when the provider shares it and the requested scope permits it).
  • Connected platform authorization: access tokens/refresh tokens, authorization scopes, channel/page identifiers, and configuration metadata required to connect and operate integrations (stored to provide the service and secured as sensitive data).
  • Runtime and workspace configuration: settings you provide for automations, triggers, moderation rules, quotas/tallies, and other operational preferences.
  • Service usage and diagnostics: feature usage, performance metrics, error reports, audit records of administrative actions, and reliability telemetry.
  • Content and community interaction data: data processed from third-party platforms when StreamSuites is used for livestream operations (for example usernames/handles, chat messages, timestamps, message IDs, moderation events, and platform identifiers). This may include information about people who do not have a StreamSuites account.
  • Media and exports: assets you upload, generated exports, configuration snapshots, and operational artifacts produced by the runtime (such as JSON manifests, logs, or reports).
  • Payments and billing data: subscription status, plan tier, invoice references, transaction timestamps, country/region, and a payment-provider customer ID. Payment card details are processed by our payment provider and are not stored by StreamSuites.
  • Communications: emails or messages you send to support, privacy requests, and service emails we send to you (including delivery metadata such as send time and delivery status).
  • Analytics data: non-advertising website/app analytics (e.g., aggregated usage, page views, approximate location derived from IP, and device/browser characteristics), subject to your choices and applicable law.
  • Status subscriptions: email address and/or phone number (if provided) when you subscribe to incident/maintenance notifications.

OAuth, third-party logins, and connected platforms

StreamSuites supports authentication and account connection flows through OAuth providers. When you use an OAuth login or connect an external account, the provider shares information with us based on the permissions (scopes) you grant.

  • OAuth providers used for sign-in and/or account linking: Google, GitHub, Discord, X, and Twitch.
  • What we typically receive: a provider user ID and basic profile details; email address only if the provider shares it and the scope permits it.
  • What we store: the provider ID, linkage metadata, and (when required for operations) tokens/scopes to access connected features you enable.
  • Scope minimization: we request only the permissions needed to operate the features you choose to enable.
  • Revocation: you can often revoke access from within the third-party provider’s account settings; revocation may disable connected features in StreamSuites.

Third-party providers process your information under their own policies. You should review the privacy settings and notices provided by each provider.

Cookies, sessions, and similar technologies

StreamSuites uses cookies and similar technologies to keep you signed in, secure the service, and measure performance. We do not use third-party advertising cookies to serve ads based on your activity.

  • Strictly necessary cookies: session cookies (often HTTP-only) used for authentication, CSRF protection, and security.
  • Security and abuse prevention: network and bot-mitigation signals may be set by our security/CDN providers to protect the service.
  • Analytics cookies: may be used for non-advertising analytics to understand site performance and usage trends (subject to your choices and applicable law).
  • Your controls: you can block or delete cookies in your browser settings. Some features may not function without necessary cookies.

Where required by law, we rely on consent for certain analytics cookies and processing.

How we use information

We use information to operate StreamSuites, secure it, and provide the features you request.

  • Provide account creation, authentication, and session management.
  • Enable dashboard functionality, runtime connectivity, and feature configuration.
  • Operate integrations you authorize (including chat automation, moderation tooling, and platform connectivity).
  • Process subscriptions, donations, invoices, refunds, and payment confirmations.
  • Send service messages (verification, security alerts, operational notices, support replies).
  • Monitor performance, debug errors, and improve reliability and usability.
  • Protect against fraud, abuse, and unauthorized access; enforce policies and legal terms.
  • Maintain audit trails for administrative actions and service integrity.
  • Produce aggregated, non-identifying analytics and reporting to understand service usage.
  • Comply with legal obligations and respond to lawful requests.

Legal bases for processing

Depending on your location, applicable law may require us to identify legal bases for processing personal information.

  • Contract: to provide the service you request (account access, runtime features, subscriptions, support).
  • Legitimate interests: to secure the service, prevent abuse, maintain reliability, and improve operations (balanced against your rights).
  • Consent: where required (for example, certain analytics cookies or optional communications).
  • Legal obligation: to meet accounting, tax, compliance, or lawful request requirements.

How we share information

We share information only as needed to operate StreamSuites and comply with law. We do not sell personal information.

  • Service providers (processors): infrastructure hosting, email delivery, payments, analytics, incident communication, monitoring, and security tooling.
  • OAuth / platform providers: when you authenticate or authorize integrations, information is exchanged as part of that connection.
  • Legal and safety: to comply with legal obligations, enforce terms, and protect users and the public.
  • Business operations: in the event of a corporate restructuring, merger, acquisition, or asset transfer (subject to applicable safeguards).

Where required, we use contractual safeguards and limit providers to processing information on our instructions.

Key third-party services used in StreamSuites operations

StreamSuites relies on third-party services to deliver the platform. These providers may process personal information on our behalf (for example, IP address, device information, email addresses, transaction metadata, and operational logs).

  • Cloudflare (CDN, DNS, security, bot mitigation): helps deliver public pages and APIs, improve performance, and protect against malicious traffic. Cloudflare may process network metadata (such as IP address, request headers, and security signals) and may set security-related cookies.
  • Hetzner (hosting / VPS / cloud infrastructure): hosts application workloads and data storage for StreamSuites runtime-connected services. Data may be processed in datacenters used to operate the service (including outside your country/region depending on deployment).
  • Resend (transactional email delivery): delivers critical emails such as verification messages, security notifications, and operational communications. Resend processes recipient and message metadata (and message content as necessary to transmit the email).
  • Stripe (payments): processes subscription payments and optional one-off donations. Stripe processes payment card data and transaction details. StreamSuites receives payment status, billing references, and customer/subscription identifiers, but does not store full card numbers.
  • Google Analytics (non-advertising analytics): used to understand aggregated usage and performance. We configure analytics for measurement and service improvement purposes, not for selling data or ad-targeting through StreamSuites.
  • Atlassian Statuspage (service status monitoring): provides incident/maintenance communications and subscription notifications. If you subscribe, Statuspage may process your email address and/or phone number for notifications.
  • OAuth identity / platform providers: Google, GitHub, Discord, X, and Twitch provide authentication and/or authorization data when you choose to use those sign-in/connection methods.

We may also use additional vendors for logging, error tracking, security monitoring, and operational tooling consistent with this policy.

Payments, subscriptions, and donations

StreamSuites uses Stripe to process paid subscriptions and optional one-time donations. Payment details are handled by Stripe and stored by Stripe under its security standards and policies.

  • What Stripe processes: payment card details, billing details, transaction amounts, and fraud-prevention signals (as applicable).
  • What StreamSuites stores: subscription tier/status, renewal dates, invoice references, and a Stripe customer/subscription identifier.
  • Chargebacks and compliance: we may retain transaction records as required for accounting, tax, and dispute resolution.

Service communications and email delivery

We send important communications related to your account and service operations (for example: login verification, security alerts, billing notices, runtime operational notices, and support responses). We use Resend for email delivery, which processes recipient information and delivery metadata.

  • Verification and security: emails to confirm your address and protect your account.
  • Operational messages: incident communications and service changes that may affect reliability or access.
  • Support: messages you send to support may be stored and processed to resolve your request.
  • Unsubscribe: some informational emails may include unsubscribe controls; critical service/security emails may be required to operate the service.

Analytics and SEO measurement

We use Google Analytics to measure performance and understand aggregated usage (for example: which pages are visited, load times, and general device/browser statistics). We use analytics to improve StreamSuites and maintain reliability.

  • No advertising targeting via StreamSuites: analytics is used for measurement and service improvement rather than ad delivery.
  • Controls: you can block analytics cookies in your browser settings, use privacy extensions, or use available Google controls.
  • Retention: analytics retention settings may be configured to limit how long user-level data is stored, consistent with product capabilities and applicable law.

Status monitoring and incident notifications

StreamSuites uses Atlassian Statuspage to communicate service incidents and maintenance. You can view status without subscribing. If you subscribe, Statuspage processes your contact details to send notifications.

  • Subscription data: email address and/or phone number (if provided) for incident/maintenance alerts.
  • Preferences: notification preferences and subscription status may be stored by Statuspage.
  • Embedded status: status information may be embedded on StreamSuites pages to keep users informed.

Retention and deletion

We retain information as long as needed to provide StreamSuites and for legitimate operational purposes.

  • Account data: retained while your account is active, and deleted or anonymized upon verified request, subject to legal/operational requirements.
  • Logs and security records: retained for a limited period for abuse prevention, auditing, and reliability analysis.
  • Billing records: retained as required for tax/accounting, disputes, and compliance.
  • Backups: backups may retain information for a limited time until overwritten through normal backup cycling.

Deletion typically begins with disabling access and then removing data from active systems, followed by backup expiry according to retention schedules.

Security

We implement technical and organizational measures designed to protect information against unauthorized access, loss, misuse, and alteration. No system can be guaranteed to be 100% secure.

  • Encryption in transit (TLS) for service communications.
  • Access controls, least-privilege administration, and audit logging for privileged actions.
  • Protection against malicious traffic and abuse via network security tooling.
  • Secure handling of sensitive tokens and secrets used for connected platform operations.

International transfers

StreamSuites and its service providers may process information in multiple countries. Where required, we use appropriate safeguards for cross-border transfers, such as contractual protections and vendor compliance commitments.

Your rights and choices

Depending on your location, you may have rights regarding personal information.

  • Access: request a copy of your personal information.
  • Correction: request updates to inaccurate or incomplete information.
  • Deletion: request deletion of your account and associated personal information (subject to retention requirements).
  • Portability: request export of certain data in a structured format where applicable.
  • Objection / restriction: object to or restrict certain processing where applicable.
  • Consent withdrawal: withdraw consent for processing that is based on consent (where applicable).
  • Cookies: control cookies via browser settings; blocking necessary cookies may break sign-in and other features.
  • OAuth connections: revoke third-party authorizations at the provider to disable connected features.

Children

StreamSuites is not directed to children under 13 (or the minimum age required by your local law). We do not knowingly collect personal information from children in a manner requiring parental consent.

Changes to this policy

We may update this policy to reflect operational, legal, or technical changes. We will revise the “Last updated” date at the top of this page when changes are published.

Contact

  • Email: privacy@streamsuites.app
  • Support: support@streamsuites.app

If you contact us, we may request information to verify your identity before acting on certain requests.
