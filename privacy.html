Privacy policy

Static surface

StreamSuites limits data collection on public pages. Creator authentication happens in the dashboard. This page summarizes what we collect, how we use it, and your choices.

Data we collect

When you create a StreamSuites account, we collect basic account identifiers and login signals needed to run the service.

  • Account data: email address, display name, and a hashed password if you use a direct login.
  • OAuth data: provider IDs from Google, GitHub, and Discord (plus the associated email if the provider shares it).
  • Operational data: login timestamps, IP address, user agent, and security events for abuse prevention.
  • Public pages: static HTML with basic server and CDN logs for uptime and performance.

Authentication, sessions, and storage

We use secure, HTTP-only session cookies to keep you signed in to the dashboard. Account data and workspace settings are stored in our database.

  • OAuth providers supported today: Google, GitHub, and Discord.
  • Session cookies are used for authentication and are not used for advertising.
  • Dashboard secrets and tokens remain in the protected admin area, not on public pages.

Access, admin visibility, and your rights

Authorized StreamSuites administrators may access account records to provide support, resolve abuse, and maintain the service.

  • You can request access to your account data or ask for corrections.
  • You can request account deletion or an onboarding reset that clears setup state.
  • Contact us at privacy@stream.suites for privacy requests.

Retention, deletion, and security

We keep account data while your account is active and retain limited logs for security and reliability.

  • Deletion requests trigger a soft-delete period before data is fully removed from active systems.
  • Backups may retain data for a limited time while they cycle out.
  • We use industry-standard security practices, but no system is guaranteed to be 100% secure.
